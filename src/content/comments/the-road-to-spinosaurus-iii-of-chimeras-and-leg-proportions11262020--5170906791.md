<p>The fact that it is an "unnamed collector" is itself a difference. I've worked with lots of local collectors in different countries, and while many of them are great people the incentives on them as they collect and sell to buyers (hobbyists as well as professionals) are not the same as someone trained and paid to work in the field for a museum (of course both can make mistakes). Composites are worth more to buyers, as is telling paleontologists what they want to hear. I saw this repeatedly with private collectors in China. Now I don't know this person from Morocco (obviously), but that's also the whole point - everything in the scaling of the torso to the rear part of FSAC depends on 3 vertebrae collected by someone who isn't on the papers and isn't identified. Which is their prerogative (and they might have solid reasons), but it doesn't exactly make the association seem more plausible. Also, the unnamed collector sold the parts to two different unrelated groups of buyers - does that strike you as the actions of someone who thought all their material was an associated individual?</p>
