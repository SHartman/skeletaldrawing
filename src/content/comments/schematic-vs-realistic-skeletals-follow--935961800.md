<p>@Mickey - I'm not trying to convince you that schematics are superior, I'm trying to convince you to start first with encouraging authors to universally adopt proper labeling.<br><br>In the long run I think it's preferable for skeletals to be realistic, but it's not fair (or realistic) to expect authors to adopt that convention when there's no real guidelines for how to achieve it.<br><br>As for Martinez &amp; Alcober's Panphagia - my guess is they simply never considered it at all, like most authors in the history of the field.  Asking for authors to label their diagrams will raise awareness of the issue (i.e., if they want a realistic skeletal they need to put in the effort to get one) and reduce systemic errors that are created when others assumed an unlabeled skeletal is realistic.</p>
