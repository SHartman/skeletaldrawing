<p>I have now been able to buy the article itself along with the free supplementary information. The additional figures were helpful. I do appologize for the comments about the Bajariasaurus femur though. Seeing the paper propers actual scale drawing of the actual femur of the neotype ( as oppposed to the verbal description in the supplementary information) shows that it is significantly different from that bone in Stromers (1934, Plate III, fig. 14). It also looks as though predatory dinosaurs are rather uniform in the number of cervicals and dorsals in their presacral vertebral column. Advanced birdlike forms like Avimimus gain one cervical at most. (I consider vertebrae to be dorsals even if they are anterior to those that connect to the sternum as long as they function as part of the trunk and not the neck.</p>
