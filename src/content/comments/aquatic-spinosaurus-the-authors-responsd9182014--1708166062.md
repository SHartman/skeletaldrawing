<p>If Spinosaurus weighed 8 to 10 tons, I reckon I'd eat my hat- sure its a big animal, but looking at the new restoration compared to Tyrannosaurus which it  is now supposed to be bigger than, it has:</p><p>1. A longer tail</p><p>2. A longer neck</p><p>3.A long lightly built skull</p><p>4. A shallow torso</p><p>5. If its to be believed, a pair of puny legs ( compared to T.rex)</p><p>All this adds up in my opinion to an animal that might have weighed 5 or 6 tons.</p><p>Now about those legs, if evolution had done its thing to reduce the size of the rear legs, bring on pachyostosis and other changes to accomodate a more aquatic lifestyle, why didn't it change its forelimbs to be more useful for locomotion- swimming or walking?Knuckle or wrist walking? Its the worst of both worlds not much good for swimming and not much good for walking unlike for example a sea lion which has at least one of those bases covered. How about the large thumb claw? A good gaff for fishing, but of what use if the animal would have had to use the forelimbs for locomotion? I don't think it would have had the strength to use one arm to hold a fish and the other to stand on the back of the wrist.</p><p>I've never doubted the piscivorous lifestyle, but those legs....</p>
