<p>@Jaimie - I agreed with much of what you wrote in the first post on this subject.  But just because all illustrations are to some degree schematic is not at all the same as saying that there's no difference between a skeletal that is properly scaled and accurately reproduces the outlines of bones, and those which do not take care to attempt this.  My biggest concern right now is that people label their intentions in professional articles, so as not to accidentally mislead readers.<br><br>The ultimate issue of all skeletals being illustrations is why I use the term "realistic" rather than "real" or "literal".  Regardless, a realistic skeletal is a very different beast than a schematic one is, and they ought to be labeled, just like any salient data in a figure or diagram.</p>
