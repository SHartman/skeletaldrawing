<p>I often wonder how much of what we interperate as say a crest, or feathers pointing out perpendicular to the skeleton can be attributed to the fact that the animals are flattened? When we see dead birds their feathers are splayed out all over the place. Not at all like what we see when they are alive. I've always thought it would be an interesting study to do something akin to the body farms of forensic pathologists replacing the humans with birds. I often wonder if we would glean anything from trying to recreate the conditions of burial etc. of the feathered dinosaur specimens to try and understand if what we see would have been a feature when the animal was alive.</p>
