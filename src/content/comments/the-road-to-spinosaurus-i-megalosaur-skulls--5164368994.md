<p>Ironically, the only known nasals for megalosauroid (or megalosauroid-grade) taxa are in spinosaurids and Monolophosaurus, and both show an elongate midline nasal crest. So, the actual evidence is for that condition and not the crestless one. <br>Pending a megalosaurid nasal, of course.</p><p>My comment on the meme stems from the stereotype surrounding megalosauroids as "primitive carnosaurs" thus "unspecialized carnosaurus" thus "unornamented carnosaurs" then "crestless Megalosauroids.</p>
