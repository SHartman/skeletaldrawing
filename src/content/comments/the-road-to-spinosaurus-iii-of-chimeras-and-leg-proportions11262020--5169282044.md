<p>It is also noteworthy that Russell (1996), based on vertebral material, suggested 3 distinct species of "Sigilmassasaurus" (=Spinosaurinae indet.) based on two morphotypes from Morocco and the "Spinosaurus B" material from Egypt. He suggested they form an evolutionary series: given how bad is the stratigraphy of most of North African sites, we cannot exclude that these specimens pertain to distinct taxa placed in distinct moments along the "mid Cretaceous" of North Africa. This would support different morphologies and proportions, and reject the use of all of them for a single taxon.</p><p>PS: "comparing apples to oranges" is also used in Italian ;-)</p>
