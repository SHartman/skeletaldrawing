<p>Am I correct in recalling that Evers et al. tentatively referred "Spinosaurus B" to Sigilmassasaurus as opposed to Spinosaurus, based on it's vertebrae? I don't know what your views on Sig/Spino and that whole associated can of worms are, but if there's multiple species of spinosaurine scattered throughout North Africa I'm not sure "Spinosaurus B" is the safest choice to base Spinosaurus proper on. Not that it's a bad choice but if there's doubt about it being more closely allied to the S. aegyptiacus type than the FSAC specimen is then it's usefulness as a reference declines.</p><p>Also if several parts of the neotype are all roughly of the same age then that lends credence to them all belonging to specimens of very similar size at the very least even if we don't accept that they are outright the same specimen.</p><p>I'm curious what your thoughts would be on vertebrae positions. Some 4 years ago Getawaytrike produced a much longer-legged (but still stumpy) Spinosaurus merely by shifting the various FSAC dorsal centra back 3 positions but I don't know enough myself to investigate the merits of doing this.</p>
