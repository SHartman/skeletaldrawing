<p>It's hard to assess the exact phylogenetic position of Abydosaurus based on the skull and anterior cervicals (i.e. all that's well published) since those elements aren't known in most brachiosaurs (except of course Giraffatitan), and certainly aren't known in Cedarosaurus, but it's pretty close in time and geography, so it seems like the most reasonable proxy skull at this point.</p>
