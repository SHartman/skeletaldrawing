<p>Functionally? Not that much different. There is no way to determine the type of nerve ending or sensory organ at the end of the nerve fiber from the fossils. The number of openings and their concentration in an area of the snout may be more useful, as it allows us to speculate that more narves are running to certain regions of the snout than others, and this has some import.</p>
