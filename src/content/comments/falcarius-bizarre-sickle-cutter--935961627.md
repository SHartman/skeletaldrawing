<p>The best answer I can give you for how Greg came up with that length was that he assumed that a mid-tail section from a smaller individual was the correct size. Having seen the sacrum and other caudals all I can say is they aren't that small.<br><br>There aren't any fully articulated tails, so the caudal count is based on its phylogenetic position - there's no justification for such a high count (which would obviously be homoplastic).<br><br>As for balance, the shorter tail that I restore actually is more massive than Greg's, so you aren't losing anything in terms of balance. The neck, as you say, is not very dense, and as I mentioned the pelvic canal of Falcarius is already enlarged quite a bit, so the mass in the abdomen has already begun to shift backwards.<br><br>Despite all that, the center of gravity is just behind the right foot, not under the pelvis. But there isn't any theropod that has its center of gravity below the hips, so that's not strange either.</p>
