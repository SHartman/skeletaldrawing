<p>The main point would be that Spinosaurus's closest relatives like Baryonyx and Suchomimus simply <em>do not</em> have that extreme of limb proportions. In other words, the whole "whale-like" evolutionary line, itself, is debatable. We have <em>definitive</em> evidence of modern-day cetaceans being descended from land-based mammals because of both modern whale and dolphin skeletons, plus the fossils of Ambulocetus, Dorudon, the beast I like to call "Basilocetus" because, well, you know, etc.</p><p>I'm just saying, in order for the "baby-leg" hypothesis for Spinosaurus to be plausible, we need to find ancestors that have at least some signs of having shrinking legs over time, in other words, the spinosaurid equivalent of Ambulocetus.</p><p>This reconstruction doesn't solve the problem as much as raising new ones.</p>
