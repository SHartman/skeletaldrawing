<p>I think 6.5 is a bit long for the known specimens - remember that those length estimates were usually assuming Deinonychus-like tail proportions. Since Utahraptor seems to have a short tail, we might be closer to 5 meters for the known specimens. But in the case of a 6.5 meter long specimen 700+ kg sounds about right.</p>
