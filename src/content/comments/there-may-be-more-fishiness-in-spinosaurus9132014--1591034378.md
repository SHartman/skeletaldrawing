<p>That is an important observation about the femur, That is about where the above photo departs  in shape from the paper propers figure 2 (above the prominent anterior trochanter). In figure 2 the tibia is longer than the femur. Do you have E. Stromers 1915 paper?, I have seen your photograph and the centra do look taller in it and not as long. However all of the centra in the destroyed type specimen were illustrated separate by a small space from their neural arches. It is clear that Stromers type specimen was itself a subadult! The centra do not look that much different than those of Spinosaurus B, or the dorsal centra of Sigilmassasaurus. In the photo the vertebrae are cleaned up and the centra are mated to their neural arches along rather strait seams, I wonder if this is an artifact of the cleanup process they went through before display.</p>
