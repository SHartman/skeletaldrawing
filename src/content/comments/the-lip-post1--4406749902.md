<p>It's nowhere close to 7 inches - like most theropod skulls the teeth of tyrannosaurs frequently slip out (or are prepped out) of their sockets in death, and the over-sized teeth you see in many casts are simply that (some casts seem to have teeth purposely inserted with huge exposed roots back into empty sockets, presumably to make it look toothier). The resulting 4-5 inches of tooth crowns may have been able to slip somewhat into the lower jaw tooth pocket, further reducing the necessary lip size. Also, Morhardt's measurements show that tyrannosaurs actually have more foramina on average than normal theropods, not less.</p><p>And finally, there simply IS a smooth, sharp edge on the maxilla of tyrannosaurs below the row of foramina. You can see it in the photos I posted here, or by Googling it. It's admittedly not as extensive as we see in many lizards, but it's also not at all like extant crocodilians.</p>
