<p>Anguilliform swimming seems to occur in small organisms but not in large ones.<br>I wonder if there is some size related feature in the hydrodynamics and drag that makes an anguilliform shape less efficient above some body length threshold.<br>Crocodilians use an elongated flattened tail to swim but they can get very close to their prey by approaching underwater; at very close range the sudden acceleration they can achieve is important but it does not occur over any great distance nor reach a very high speed where drag would be important.<br>However the only truly marine specialists in the crocodilians, the metriorhynchids which fed on aquatic prey developed a reversed heterocercal tail.</p><p>Also fish that accelerate towards their prey rapidly like pike or barracuda still have an appreciable tail fin although they do have a long body.</p>
