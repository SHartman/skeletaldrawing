<p>No worries! I work on aero and hydrodynamics so I figured I'd chip in my two cents. Even without laterally flattened bodies, I expect that a mosasaur could pull off a vertical breach - it just wouldn't be as good at it as fish that are specialist jumpers. Arowana can manage more than a body length of vertical height out of the water. By contrast, large whales breach about 1/2-2/3 of their body above the surface. The giant JW mosasaur (if it had been real) could probably manage the same.</p><p>There also remains the question of the overall body shape in mosasaurs. While they were probably pretty wide gauge up front, they might have had laterally flattened bodies from the hips back, which would give them quite a bit of fast start surface to work with. Alternatively, they might have been really fusiform most of the way back, with only the tail flattened (that seems to be the preferred reconstruction from mosasaur specialists right now).</p>
