<p>Your suggested new standardization seems quite reasonable to me.  I think that your incredulity regarding the sprinting pose is not only well founded for many taxa, but also raises an additional issue: it is distracting from the point of the reconstruction if the viewer spends time trying to work out if the pose is realistic.  Ideally, a "standard" pose should be a 'no-brainer' for most taxa, so that viewers can focus on, you know, the <em>skeleton</em>.  <br><br>There is similar issue with pterosaurs, actually.  The standard set by Greg Paul was to show pterosaurs in the middle of a bird-like running launch.  Since it is unlikely that the ever did this (or even could), those images have the problem of showing an implausible behavior for the sake of standardization.</p>
