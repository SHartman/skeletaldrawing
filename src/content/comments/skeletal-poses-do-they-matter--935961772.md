<p>I like any skeletal reconstruction that is not the norm. Not only is it refreshing from the usual "Paul-esque" format but it allows you to make your own judgments regarding how the different elements may have worked together and helps you to think out of the box, so to speak, when it comes to life restoration.<br><br>There is definitely some mileage in an exploded skeletal diagram and Heinrich's mid-way suggestion could be a great place to start.</p>
