<p>I think that the key here is bone density. The recent findings seem to indicate that Spinosaurus had much denser, less pneumatic bones than other large theropods like T. rex. Size-wise, I'd max out T. rex between 12 and 13 meters, and Spinosaurus around 15 tops (none of the 17 or 18 meter nonsense some fanboys like to spew out). If we weigh said Tyrannosaurus rex at around 6 or 7 tons, then I could see a Spinosaur of similar size with denser bones feasibly approaching 9 or 10 tons, especially if it were a largely aquatic animal.</p>
