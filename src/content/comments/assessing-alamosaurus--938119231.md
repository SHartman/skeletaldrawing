<p>Actually I did scale those, and I did not find it to be as large. Of the three specimens described (isolated partial cervical, caudal, and femur fragment) the caudal was from the largest of the three individuals, and the femur was the smallest. The idea that the cervical from Sullivan and Fowler was bigger than puertasaurus was due to the width of the cotyle, but with an actual neck to check it against (the Perot specimen) it looks like Alamosaurus just had wide cotyles.</p>
