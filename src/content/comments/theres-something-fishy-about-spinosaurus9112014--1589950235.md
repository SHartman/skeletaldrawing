<p>I do not want to blast a reputable scientist. This, however is not the first time a dinosaur has been possibly altered for reasons of "becoming a superlative". In 1996, I viewed the reconstruction of Charcharodonotsaurus, as well as the reconstructed skull of Giganotosaurus. The Charcharodontosaurus had an excessive angle to the post orbital, thereby increasing the overall length of the skull.<br>I cannot see the reasoning behind the hind limbs of a juvenile being paired with an adult skeleton, but it does indeed draw attention and further funding to one's operation.<br>All other Spinosaurids do not have this shrunken hind limb anatomy. Why would Spinosaurus deviate so much from the rest of its relatives?</p>
