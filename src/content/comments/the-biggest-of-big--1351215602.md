<p>Another giant you may want to consider is Sauroposeidon, which is widely sited as the tallest dinosaur - I personally wonder how heavy they would be, as they may be considered as a intermediate form between the bulky late titanosaurs and the "gracile" branchiosaurs. Another candidate to be included in this club might be the Mamenchisaurus. Similar to the case of Diplodocus, these early sauropods are not particularly heavy due to their weird body proportion. But the length of M. sinocanadorum is the stuff of legend, and I believe their fossils are rather complete.</p>
