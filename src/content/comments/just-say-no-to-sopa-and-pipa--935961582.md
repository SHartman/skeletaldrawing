<p>Australia is attempting to introduce similar legislation to block 'objectionable material', making it the responsibility of ISPs to do the blocking. While the target was valid, an attempt to stop pedophilia material and other truly horrible stuff, the implementation is clumsy and relies upon lists of 'objectionable' URLs provided by government. <br>The scheme is still optional and has changed in form 3 times as each successive policy has met substantial resistance from industry and open internet groups.<br>It also has similar implications to SOPA, though under a different jurisdiction, but already some objectional sites on the list had turned out to be more political in nature than anything else.</p>
