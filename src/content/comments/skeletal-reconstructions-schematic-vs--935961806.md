<p>@anonymous: It's an interesting question; the short answer is "sometimes".  Some diapsids seem to have a decent amount of cartilage in their joints, others less so.  Looking at dinosaurs specifically some joints appear much better "finished" than others.  The problem is that no one has done much to quantify it, outside of papers saying that "it could be as much as X%".  <br><br>In the case of say sauropods there might be enough cartilage to actually need to draw a gap in there, but even so when I measure the gap that some people have tried to add in their illustrations I see they are adding &gt;10% to the length of the limb bones, which is definitely at (or above) the maximum likely amount of cartilage.<br><br>It will certainly be a topic (or more likely, several topics) of future posts though.</p>
