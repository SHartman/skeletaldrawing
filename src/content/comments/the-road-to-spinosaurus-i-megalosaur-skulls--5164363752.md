<p>Thanks for the comment Andrea, I don't think it's annoying. I have no problem with the idea of a midline crest in megalosaurs, but I'm not convinced "nasal crests bracket megalosaurs" is the slam dunk you imply, as some taxa (e.g. allosaurs) lack midline nasal crests, and the distribution of and developmental relationship between midline and paired crests are not clear at this point. The developmental assumptions one makes about whether paired crests evolve into midlines crests (or vis versa) or evolve from crestless ancestors strongly influences what you think about the phylogenetic constraints on megalosaurs, since basal ceratosaurs and allosaurs have quite different nasal crests. Also, we do have plenty of megalosaur lacrimals and their ornamentation is strongly reduced compared to Ceratosaurus and basal allosaurs. There's also a taphonomic argument to consider - midline nasal crests are often among the more robust parts of a skull, which would make it all the more strange that we have no nasals from non-spinosaur megalosaurs to date.</p><p>I would hardly be surprised if we find megalosaur skulls with midline crests. But given the evidence of reduced ornamentation in other facial bones and the lack of what ought to be fairly robust nasals if they form a midline crest I don't feel the evidence is overwhelmingly in favor of it either. Hopefully more complete skulls are forthcoming!</p>
