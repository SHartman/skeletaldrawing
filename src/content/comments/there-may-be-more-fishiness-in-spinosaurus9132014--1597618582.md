<p>You're welcome. I doubt truly "underwater" bipedal locomotion occurred (even with solid bones bipedal locomotion would be much slower than swimming) but I would agree that walking through waist high waters may have been the norm. And note that I'm not ruling out belly-sliding ever, I just don't see evidence that they were specialized for that type of locomotion on a habitual basis.</p>
