<p>They confirmed that the particular bones they sliced are in the subadult range, which increases the odds. I think this is mostly a defense against the idea that FSAC is an amalgamation of many different individuals (which would then be most likely to be made up of different ontogenetic stages). But "subadult" dinosaurs are far and away the most commonly recovered ontogenetic stage in non-avialan dinosaurs, and for several reasons it's not plausible to generate an actual "how many years old" analysis from all of the bones, so it doesn't really change what I wrote above.</p>
