<p>There is something chimerical about the new reconstruction. It's difficult for me to imagine how the Ibrahim spinosaurus could have possibly survived for 15 million years in a large predator (terrestrial &amp; aquatic) rich environment. The front limbs must have been very, very strong, but they don't pronate, so that makes terrestrial locomotion unfeasible. The shortened hind limbs would have made the animal sluggish, both in land and in water. It just strikes me as unfeasible.</p>
