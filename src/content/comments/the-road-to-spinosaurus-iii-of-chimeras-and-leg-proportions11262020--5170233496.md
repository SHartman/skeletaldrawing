<p>I suspect the taxonomy is going to need revisions for some time. It could well turn out that Sigil is real and Spino B is closer to it. Or maybe there are several species across north Africa (and until we get a hold on the stratigraphic correlations, we might be looking at slightly different times for some of the material). I'll revise again if the taxonomy warrants it, but that would probably require more specimens, so perhaps it will be moot by then anyhow. As for the ontogenetic thing - subadult dinosaurs are far and away the most common ontogenetic stage collected. Moreover, they sampled the femur, tibial, a rib and a gastralia, which doesn't address the key issue of the dorsal vertebrae.</p>
