<p>Good questions. The images are taken in the dark with a long exposure and a filter over the camera to reduce non-fluorescing light sources. So in that sense they are actual "colors" and presumably reflect different chemical compositions in the rock (or glue, in some cases).</p><p>I would assume that there was a patagial tendon present (that's what gives the it the shape it has in life), but that's not something that we can observe directly with this method (or any other method that I'm aware of). Still, I think it's a reasonable functional inference.</p>
