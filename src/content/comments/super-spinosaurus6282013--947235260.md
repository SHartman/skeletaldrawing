<p>Contra Therrien &amp; Henderson (2007) there's no way to make the skull of MSNM V4047 150-160cm long, that'll make the posterior part of the skull comically small, I've seen people try, what is preserved of the rostrum is already 98cm long and is missing the most posterior parts, it's just not possible and by the way, the skull at 175cm is already based on Irritator.</p><p>That Carrano et al. (2012) found the femur to be 136.5cm instead of 143cm has no effect on the overall size of the animal, Hartman doesn't scale the whole animal on a single bone, the same goes for the skull, also we have to know what Carrano et al (2012) means by skull length, they said that just by giving the skull of SMG DIN-1 a premaxilla based on Giganotosaurus and Mapusaurus it'll make it 142cm long, using the illustrations in Sereno et al. (1996) I can say with confidence that what Carrano et al. (2012) means by skull length is more commonly known as dorsal skull length or condylobasal skull length, making the premaxilla more like giganotosaurus like they said reduced that measurement to exactly 142cm but the greatest length was 155cm (instead of 160cm), I'm pretty sure that the same goes for Giganotosaurus ~1.4m dorsal skull length and 1.53cm greatest skull length.</p>
