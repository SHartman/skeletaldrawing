<p>Unless I'm mistaken, in 1914 they went in the field together after having known one another for over a decade. And despite Markgraf getting sick on that expedition Stromer continued to hire him. I've always seen the later excavations by Markgraf as something like the Sternberg situation, where you hire a proven and field-tested collector to excavate things for you. I'm certainly not trying to attack commercial collecting over all, but I've seen in person how local upstarts without training collect things in China (and Wyoming, for that matter), and the description in Evers seems to match. I am also not attacking those people - finding a way to support yourself or you family is 100% an understandable motivation. But it's not the sort of collecting I'd want to trust an otherwise extraordinary association of bones to.</p>
