<p>I would suggest using EDXRF to destructively look at the bones. I did this at the Dinosaur center last summer to compare TYA, FS, BB, and other sites. Unsurprisingly, some sites had quite different trace elements (both presence and absence and quantity) than others. Presumably a skeleton that comes from the same animal and fossilized via the same diagenetic event will have the same rare element tracers (Uranium, Yttrium, Lead, Arsenic, etc) that could be ratioed to provide an ID for the site. We do this kind of thing all the time with stone tools in archaeology. It isn't a guarantee, but it at least would be an empirical way to approach these kinds of problems from a different angle.</p>
