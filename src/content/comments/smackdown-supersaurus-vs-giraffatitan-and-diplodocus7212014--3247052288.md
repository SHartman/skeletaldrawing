<p>"It's scaled to be a large well-fed specimen of 30 meters and around ~170 tonnes, far from the largest individual ever reported.</p><p>Actually, that is very close to the record. The average blue whale weighs between 113-130 tons, and is 79-84 feet long. The 209 ton specimen was not verified. The largest verified was 191 tons. And don't forget to take into account the type of body tissue when talking about weight vs. volume. Blue whales have a very thick layer of blubber around them, whereas a much larger percentage of the sauropods' mass will be lean body mass.</p>
