<p>I am surprised by the thin base of the tail… did the caudemofemoralis muscles become diminished? I do wonder if this was something peculiar to these particular paravians and how much we can extend that look to other dinosaurs? Glad to see mention of "fat" ankles in Anchiornis and possibly other dinosaurs in this blog post by Scott. I've been drawing my dinosaurs with nice fat kankles for a while. I suspect something allometric was going on… that with increased mass cartilage and soft tissue at the ankle and other joints increased to act as shock absorbers at large size. Look at the ankles of rhinos and large ground birds/ratites - they are chunky knobby masses!!</p>
