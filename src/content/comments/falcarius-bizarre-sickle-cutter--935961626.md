<p>As a quick addition to Scott's reply, there is actually no biomechanical requirement that balance be achieved in a bipedal animal (or any terrestrial critter, for that matter) by purely passive means - negative pitching moments can be overcome by the intrinsic back musculature.  Humans, for example, are intrinsically unstable any time we are on one foot, but our abductors keep us from falling over.  <br><br>The trick, of course, is that the muscles can only do so much - as a result, the center of gravity needs to be in a reasonable position to begin with, or else the animal is in trouble.  The cg need not be lined up exactly, however.</p>
