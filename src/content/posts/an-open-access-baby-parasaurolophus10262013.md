*Parasaurolophus* is one of the most popular of the duck-billed hadrosaurs, but fossils of the trombone-dinosaur have proven frustratingly elusive. A new paper published by Andrew Farke and coauthors adds a valuable new juvenile specimen, which sheds light on how *Parasaurolophus* was able to grow its remarkable crest. Even better, the authors have made the entire paper, along with all of its data (including 3D scan data) available to anyone who wants to use it. I also got to play a role, working with Andy to produce a skeletal reconstruction of the specimen known as ["Joe"](http://dinosaurjoe.org/).
