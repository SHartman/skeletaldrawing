I'm often asked about what I think of "transitional fossils". Here's the thing: it's a red herring. Not because we can't see important transitions everywhere in the fossil record, but because a single fossil can't show a transition *by definition*. If you want to see a transition you need a *series of related animals*, generally organized by who is related to whom.

I've taken one of the series I use for teaching and public speaking and turned it into an animated gif for you here. I realize there aren't any names on this version - I'll post a non-animated version with labels next week. In the meantime I think it shows off the more important concept: how easy it is to look at two very different structures and feel like they are insurmountably different up until you fill in other fossil relatives.
