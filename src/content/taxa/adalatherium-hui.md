_Adalatherium_ is a gondwanathere from the Late Cretaceous of Madagascar. The name _Adalatherium_ translates as "crazy beast", although if that's generating images of it viciously hunting down baby dinosaurs...you might be disappointed to know it's thought to have eaten plants, potentially digging for roots in its underground burrows. It may mollify you to know that growing to 50+ cm (20") and tipping the scales at 5 kg (11 pounds), _Adalatherium_ was among the largest mammals to live during the Mesozoic. 

Assuming, that is, _Adalatherium_ was actually a mammal. Gondwanatheres are an enigmatic group of (probably?) basal-mammals that lived in the southern hemisphere, during the latter half of the Mesozoic though the first half of the Cenozoic. Typically they have been found to be related to another extinct mammal clade, the multituberculates, united within the "different beasts, or Allotheria. But a 2020 study found them to instead group with other almost-mammals just outside of Mammalia. This may go back and forth for a while in the scientific literature, but here we will consider them mammalian allotheres.
