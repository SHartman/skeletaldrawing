A small dromaeosaurid theropod from the Late Cretaceous of western North America. _Bambiraptor_ is known from a well-preserved juvenile skeleton from Montana, which was found alongside a larger, adult specimen that was less complete and has not been described in as much detail. Although much has been made about the supposedly bird-like proportions of this winged "raptor" dinosaur, in reality it's about as avian-like in anatomy as any other dromaeosaur. 

Found in the same formation as _Saurornitholestes langstoni_, it's possible they are actually the same species, although the type material of _Saurornitholestes_ is scrappy, and more recent, more complete specimens have been suggested to differ enough to keep _Bambiraptor_ and _Saurornitholestes_ separate. If so, it would be similar to how many ecosystems have relatives of somewhat different size (e.g. foxes, coyotes and wolves living in the same ecosystem).
