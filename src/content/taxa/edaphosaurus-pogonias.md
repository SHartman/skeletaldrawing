_Edaphosaurus_ was a plant-eating, Early Permian synapsid. Living in the same time and swampy environments as its carnivorous relative _Dimetrodon_, _Edaphosaurus_ had an even stranger sail, which had little projections sticking sideways out of every elongated neural spine. Wind-tunnel tests suggest this would produce eddies of air in a breeze, causing convection currents that would aid in cooling down its body temperature.
