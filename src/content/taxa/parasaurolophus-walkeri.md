_Parasaurolophus walkeri_ is a lambeosaurine hadrosaurid (hollow-crested duck-billed dinosaur) from the Late Cretaceous of Alberta, Canada. _Parasaurolophus_ is famous for its long, backswept tubular head crest, which housed looping nasal passages that may have produced resonant calls. Despite their fame, specimens of _Parasaurolophus_ are relatively rare, suggesting they were either a minor part of their ecosystems, or potentially lived most of the year elsewhere and only passed through on occasion (thus making fewer fossils for us to find).
