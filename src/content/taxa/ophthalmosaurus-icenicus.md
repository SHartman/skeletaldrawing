A Middle-to-Late Jurassic ichthyosaur from the Oxford Clay of England, _Ophthalmosaurus_ means "eye lizard." Which is a good name, as _Ophthalmosaurus_ had enormous eyes, among the largest relative to body size of any known animal. Large eyes can gather more light, making them suited to hunting in deep, dim water.
