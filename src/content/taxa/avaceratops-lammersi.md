_Avaceratops_ was a centrosaurine horned dinosaur that lived during the Late Cretaceous, in the famous Judith River deposits of western North America. The first specimen was believed to belong to a horned dinosaur that was 2.3 m (7.5 feet) long, although recent analysis of its bones show the animal was still growing rapidly, so we might expect it to have at least reached the size of its close relative _Furcatoceratops_, which grew closer to 4 m (12 feet) in length. 

Both genera belong to a recently recognized group of centrosaurines called the Nasutoceratopsini, which includes the bull-horned _Nasutoceratops_. This group of basal centrosaurines had larger brow horns than nose horns, a distinctive look compared to later nose-horn dominant centrosaurines like _Styracosaurus_ and _Centrosaurus_.
