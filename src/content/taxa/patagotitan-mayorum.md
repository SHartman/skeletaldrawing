_Patagotitan_ was an enormous titanosaur from the Cretaceous of Argentina. It vies with _Argentinosaurus_ and an isolated shin-bone that has been referred to _Alamosaurus_ for the title of largest land animal, but the two specimens of _Patagotitan_ provide a much clearer view of the animal's skeleton than in _Argentinosaurus_ (and obviously more than a single shin bone!).  

_Patagotitan_ has had several claims made about its length and mass. It doesn't appear heavier than _Argentinosaurus_, although I haven't yet done a GDI mass estimate for it. While it has been reported to be as long as 40 m (131 feet), and the mount at the Field Museum is reported to be 37 m (122 feet) long, those estimates seem to be significantly inflated by the addition of extra vertebrae in the middle of the tail. In this reconstruction I get a length of \~28 m (92 feet), which is still a very long animal, but falls short of prior estimates.
