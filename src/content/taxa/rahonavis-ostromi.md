_Rahonavis_ was a small dromaeosaurid from the Late Cretaceous of Madagascar. _Rahonavis_ had a raised sickle claw on each foot like other dromaeosaurids, and some aspects of its pelvis and vertebral anatomy suggest it may have belonged to a peculiar group of southern hemisphere dromaeosaurs called unenlagiines, although this is not fully settled. A fun bit of trivia is that when originally described, the animal was initially given the genus name _Rahona_, but this turned out to be pre-occupied by a moth, so it was renamed _Rahonavis_. 

One open question involves the forelimb bones (the ulna and radius). When first described much was made about how large they were, and the well-developed quill knobs (raised bumps where wing feathers attach by tendon to the ulna) - a big deal in 1998, because fewer feathered dinosaurs were known. Yet the two forelimb bones are actually _very_ large compared to the rest of the skeleton, and it's worth noting that they were found only loosely associated, in a quarry that also produced more advanced, flying avialans. If the forelimbs do go with _Rahonavis_, this may be another example of incipient flight evolving independently of birds.
