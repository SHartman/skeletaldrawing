A Late Cretaceous teleost of the North American inland sea, this3 m (10 foot) predatory fish was actually a smaller relative of the giant _Xiphactinus_. Its jaws carried a single row of even, closely spaced teeth rather than the outsized fangs of its cousin, though smaller fish probably would prefer to no bump into either of them, when given a choice!
