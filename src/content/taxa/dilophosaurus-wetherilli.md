The most famous early theropod dinosaur, _Dilophosaurus_ lived during the Early Jurassic in the American southwest. Best known from Arizona, it had a distinctive pair of thin, arching crests on its skull. 

Its appearance in the book and movies of the Jurassic Park franchise made it a dinosaur celebrity, but its fame has gone hand in hand with several fictional misconceptions. First, there is no evidence of venom in _Dilophosaurus_ (which is often indicated by pits in the skull and/or teeth), let alone spitting black goo. The movies largest fictional departure is the giant, expanding frill of skin around the head and neck. Species that have this (like the frilled lizard, which inspired it) have enormously expanded hyoids and other throat elements to move and expand the frill - elements that _Dilophosaurus_ does not have. 

The frill was added to _Dilophosaurus_ in the movie to solve a specific problem - there was concern that movie-goes might mix up _Velociraptor_ and _Dilophosaurus_, especially since they had decided to enlarge their movie 'raptors to be closer to the actual size of _Dilophosaurus._ Yet they still thought people might be confused, so they shrunk the movie _Dilophosaurus_ down as well, making both theropods the wrong size. At roughly 7 meters /23 feet long, _Dilophosaurus_ was actually among the largest land predators of the Early Jurassic.
