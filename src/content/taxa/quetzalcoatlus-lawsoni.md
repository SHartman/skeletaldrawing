_Quetzalcoatlus_ was an enormous Late Cretaceous azhdarchid pterosaur (pterosaurs being flying reptiles closely related to dinosaurs, but not one of them). Such giants may flown across continents...or perhaps even between them, but so far _Quetzocoatlus_ fossils hail from Big Bend, Texas (I guess everything really is bigger there?). 

There are currently two species of _Quetzalcoatlus_, and the smaller of the two, _Q. lawsoni_ is shown here, for the very good reason that it's a heck of a lot more complete than the remains of the bigger _Q. northropi_ (for reference, _Q. lawsoni_ had a wingspan of \~5 m / 15 feet, while _Q. northropi_ had a wingspan closer to 10 m/ 30+ feet). Both species of _Quetzocoatlus_ would likely have stalked prey on the ground, walking around on their long legs, spearing small animals with their long neck and pointing, toothless beak.
