_Nothronychus_ was a plant-eating therizinosaur from the Late Cretaceous of Utah. Typical of more advanced therizinosaurs, _Nothronychus_ had a small head, long neck, short, downcurved tail, broad body and enormous hand claws. There are two fairly similar species of _Nothronychus_ described to date, the type species _N. mckinleyi,_ and the more completely preserved _N. graffami_.  and is notable for being a marine-deposited find far offshore.

The type specimen of _N. graffami_ (UMNH VP 16420, shown here) was actually found in shallow, inland sea deposits. After it died, it apparently underwent "bloat and float" transport further out into the inland sea, eventually bursting and sinking to the bottom to be buried. This resulted in a very complete specimen, missing only the skull and most of the neck. Happily, those exact parts are most well-represented in the _N. mckinleyi_ specimen, making _Nothronychus_ as a whole among the best-represented therizinosaur \s to date.
