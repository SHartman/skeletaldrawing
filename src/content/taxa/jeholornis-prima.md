An early, avialan theropod from the Early Cretaceous of China_. Jeholornis_ had large wings, and a tail fan (apparently just for display) at the end of a long, bony tail. The shoulder of _Jeholornis_ allowed a larger flapping motion than in _Archaeopteryx_, but smaller than in modern birds. The big wings were clearly used aerodynamically, but the limited flapping power and absence of a specialized flap-recovery mechanism suggests _Jeholornis_ was a limited flier. Some specimens of _Jeholornis_ preserve fossilized seeds in its gut, indicating at least a partly seed-eating diet. Since _Jeholornis_ lacked a retroverted hallux, it presumably foraged for seeds and other food on the forest floor.
