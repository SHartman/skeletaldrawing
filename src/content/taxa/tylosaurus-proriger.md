_Tylosaurus_ was a fast-swimming, "ram-shouted" member of the mosasaurs, a group of marine lizards (not dinosaurs) that had rapidly radiated to become the dominant predators in Late Cretaceous seas. While surely possessing a nasty bite, the pointed snout of _Tylosaurus_ is thought to have been used for swimming at high speed into potential prey, stunning them and making them easier to dispatch. The largest _Tylosaurus_ individuals, including the Bruce specimen (MT2) of _Tylosaurus pembinensis_ (shown here) and the Bunker specimen (which was recently given its own species, _Tylosaurus rex_), grew up to 13 meters (42 feet) in length, and potentially weighing up to 7 tonnes, these were true monsters.
