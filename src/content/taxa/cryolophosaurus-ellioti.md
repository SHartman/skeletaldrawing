An early large-bodied theropod from the Early Jurassic of Antarctica, distinguished by a peculiar bony crest rising above its eyes. The superficial similarity to a pompadour hairstyle led to its nickname "Elvisaurus" after the most famous rock and roll star to sport the hairdo.

Paleontology in Antarctica is challenging - most of the rocks are buried under ice, and the ones that are exposed are only warm enough to work on for a few weeks out of the year, so _Cryolophosaurus_ is still one of the few dinosaurs known from the continent (alongside its potential prey, the bipedal sauropodomorph _Glacialisaurus_). In the Early Jurassic, at almost 8 meters (25 feet) long, _Cryolophosaurus_ was among the largest predators of its time.
