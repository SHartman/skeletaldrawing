_Presbyornis_ is an early relative of modern waterfowl (ducks and geese) from the Paleocene and Eocene of North America. _Presbyornis_ combined a duck-like head with long, wading legs, and is known from bone beds suggesting it lived in large flocks. While _Presbyornis_ itself has only been recovered in the Cenozoic, other presbyornithids have been found in the Late Cretaceous, making them one of the earliest diverging groups of crown-birds.
