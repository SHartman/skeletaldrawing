A small feathered dinosaur from the Late Jurassic of China, about 160 million years ago. Only about 60 centimeters long, _Anchiornis_ had wing-feathers on its forelimbs and shorter wings on its hindlimbs. The fossilized feathers preserved melanosomes, which allowed _Anchiornis_ the distinction of being the first Mesozoic dinosaur to have its color reconstructed. We now know it was gray/black with a reddish crest and white-banded wings. 

Almost certainly not a flying theropod, _Anchiornis_ and its close relatives have bounced around the paravian family tree - sometimes found as basal avialans, sometimes as troodontids and sometimes as more primitive than the split between both groups. As non-fliers, resolving their phylogenetic position will help us understand how close some groups got to flight, and how many times incipient flight may have evolved.
