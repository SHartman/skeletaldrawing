For more than a century _Hypsilophodon_ was seen as the quintessential small, lightly built ornithopod dinosaur. Living during the Early Cretaceous of England, where it's best known from the Isle of Wight. _Hypsilophodon_ was once proposed to be a tree-climber, although its anatomy is more consistent with a fast-running, ground-dwelling plant-eater, roughly the size of a large dog.
