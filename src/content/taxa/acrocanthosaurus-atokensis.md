_Acrocanthosaurus_ was a large predatory dinosaur that lived during the Early Cretaceous in North America, roughly 113 million years ago. A relative of _Giganotosaurus_ and _Carcharodontosaurus_, and somewhat more distantly to _Allosaurus_, it grew to 12 (40 feet) long. _Acrocanthosaurus_ was notable for the tall neural spines running down its back, which supported either a low ridge or a muscular hump. 

While several specimens of _Acrocanthosaurus_ have been found, none of them are exceptionally complete, so this reconstruction is a composite of NCSM 14345 ("Fran"), SMU 74646, and OMNH 10146. The scale bar is based on Fran, the largest specimen known so far.
