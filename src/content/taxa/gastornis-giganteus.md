As a large, flightless ground bird from the Paleocene and Eocene of North America and Eurasia, _Gastornis_ can be seen as quickly filling in the spaces vacated by its non-avian theropod relatives after the K/Pg extinction. But which role? _Gastornis_ was traditionally seen as "the bird who ate horses", since early horse relatives were the size of dogs at the time. But turns out the massive deep beak, once assumed to be the fearsome tool of a predator, has been shown to support a plant-eating lifestyle, an interpretation that is also supported by isotopic geochemical analysis of _Gastornis_ remains.
