_Supersaurus_ is a gigantic diplodocid from the Late Jurassic Morrison Formation, and the sauropod most dear to my heart\*. Known from two specimens, the larger type specimen, BYU 9025, was excavated from a quarry full of disarticulated sauropod remains, so the description of WDC DMJ-021 helped clarify which bones belonged to _Supersaurus_ from the original site, and being more complete it also helped establish the overall proportions of _SUpersaurus._ By combining the two skeletons, it turns out the largest specimen of _Supersaurus_ grew to more than 40 meters (130 feet) long, making it not just the longest dinosaur with complete enough remains to make a reasonable length estimate, but also longer than the blue whale, making it the longest known animal to ever live. Much of that length was neck and tail, so _Supersaurus_ isn't a serious contender for the heaviest dinosaur, let alone competing with blue whales for mass. So far I have been talking about the type species, _Supersaururr vivianae_, but it's worth noting that another species of _Supersaurus_, _S. lourinhanensis_ has been described from the Late Jurassic of Portugal, although some researchers believe it deserves its own genus, _Dinheirosaurus lourinhanensis._

\*I got to work on the original dig site of WDC DMJ-021, the "Jimbo" specimen, and the description of it was a key early-career paper.
