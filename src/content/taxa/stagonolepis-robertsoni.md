_Stagonolepis_ was a Late Triassic aetosaur, a group of armored, plant-eating pseudosuchians on the crocodile-line of archosaurs. _Stagonolepis_ was the first aetosaur ever described, from bony scales Louis Agassiz mistook for a fish. Very much not a fish, _Stagonolepis_ had complex armor and a blunt, upturned pig-like snout. While long assumed to be plant-eaters, recent research has suggested aetosaurs may have pursued a more omnivorous diet.
