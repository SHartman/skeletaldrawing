_The_ Late Cretaceous titanosaur from Western North America. Found across the southwestern United States, fragmentary remains from Mexico suggested _Alamosaurus_ may have challenged as the largest land animal of all time. _Tyrannosaurus rex_ (or a close relative) lived along side, and presumably hunted _Alamosaurus_.

The reconstruction here is a composite of multiple specimens, including the complete, adult neck vertebrae from the Perot Museum of Nature and Science, specimen BIBE 45854. The length estimate is scaled to BIBE 45854.

Already not a small dinosaur, It's currently debated whether fragmentary remains from Mexico belong to this species. One partial tibia (shin bone) records a true behemoth, that would have been close to 30 meters in length, and challenged _Argentinosaurus_ and _Patagotitan_ for the largest known land animal.
