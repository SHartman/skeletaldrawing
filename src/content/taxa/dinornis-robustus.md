The South Island giant moa, a huge flightless bird endemic to New Zealand. Females of _Dinornis_ stood among the tallest birds ever known, being able to stretch their heads up to around 3.5 meters (12 feet) and tipping the scales at 250 kg (500+ pounds). In the opposite pattern often observed in mammals, the males were only half as tall and maybe a third the weight of females. _Dinornis_ survived on the South Island until human hunting drove it to extinction around 1400 AD.
