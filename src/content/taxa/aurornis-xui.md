A small, feathered paravian theropod from the Late Jurassic of Liaoning, China, probably a close relative of _Anchiornis_. Roughly crow-sized, _Aurornis_ had wings but they were too small, lacked sufficient range and were not powerfully enough muscled for flight. As such, the position of _Aurornis_ and other anchiornithids figure prominently in debates over the earliest stages of wing evolution.
