_Huaxiagnathus_ was a compsognathid theropod from the Early Cretaceous of northeastern China. Although a length of 1.7 m (6 feet) doesn't sound like a giant, it's larger than most other species considered compsognathids. It was also found with a dense covering of fur-like bristles like its smaller relative _Sinosauropteryx_, at a time when finding fuzzy and feathery non-avian dinosaurs was still rare. This was seen as something of a breakthrough in how large fuzzy theropods grew, although with the power of hindsight is seems trivial after the 9 m (30 foot) theropod _Yutyrannus_ was also found to be covered in fuzz.

Like other compsognathids, _Huaxiagnathus_ presumably chased after small prey like mammals, lizards and amphibians, where its long grasping hands may have helped catch and/or carry such prey items.
