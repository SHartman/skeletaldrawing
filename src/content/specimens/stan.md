Found near Buffalo, South Dakota in 1987 and named for Stan Sacrison, this is one of the most complete and most studied *Tyrannosaurus* skeletons. For decades its casts stood in museums worldwide, making it among the most familiar tyrannosaur skeletons on display. Long catalogued as BHI 3033, the specimen sold at auction in 2020 and is now held by the Natural History Museum Abu Dhabi under the number NHMAD 2020.00001.

The skull preserves a striking set of healed injuries — punctures and fused, re-grown bone — that have made Stan a touchstone for work on tyrannosaur paleopathology and bite mechanics.
