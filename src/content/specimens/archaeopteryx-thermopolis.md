The Thermopolis specimen (WDC-CSG-100) is among the most complete _Archaeopteryx_ skeletons known, preserving a well-articulated skull and a (somewhat controversial) hyperextensible second toe. It has figured prominently in debates over how bird-like the earliest avialans really were.
