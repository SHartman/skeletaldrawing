Discovered near Faith, South Dakota in 1990 and named for its finder, Susan Hendrickson, FMNH PR 2081 is the most complete *Tyrannosaurus rex* yet found — preserving the great majority of the skeleton, including an almost intact skull. After a protracted ownership dispute it was acquired by the Field Museum in 1997 and remains the reference specimen against which other individuals are measured.

The reconstruction is shown here in two views, with the known material below it and a muscle reconstruction further down — the same individual read three ways, from the bones actually preserved, to the restored skeleton, to the musculature draped over it.
