Discovered by rancher Kathy Wankel in 1988 on the shore of Fort Peck Reservoir, Montana, this specimen — long known as MOR 555, the "Wankel rex" — was the first *Tyrannosaurus* found with a complete forelimb, settling decades of guesswork about how short and how strongly built the arms really were. In 2014 it went on long-term loan to the Smithsonian, where it was remounted and unveiled as "The Nation's T. rex," catalogued USNM 555000.
