Collected by Barnum Brown in Montana in 1908, AMNH 5027 supplied the first essentially complete *Tyrannosaurus* skull, and the upright American Museum mount built around it defined the popular image of the animal for most of the twentieth century. Though it preserves no forelimbs and only part of the postcranium, its skull remains one of the most figured and most influential in the genus.
