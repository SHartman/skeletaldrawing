Collected by Barnum Brown in Montana in 1908, AMNH 5027 supplied the first essentially complete _Tyrannosaurus_ skull, and the upright American Museum mount built around it defined the popular image of _T. rex_ for most of the twentieth century. Though it preserves no fore or hind limbs, it has an excellent vertebral column, and its skull remains one of the better figured and most influential in the genus.
